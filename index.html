Introduction to Computer and Social Networks

What is a computer network?

A computer network is a system that connects multiple devices mainly for communication and sharing resources. These networks can be through advanced wireless technology such as Bluetooth and Wi-Fi or wired connections through physical cables. They range from small Wi-Fi setups to large scale networks such as the internet.

What is its purpose?

  • Easy Communication
  • Resource Sharing
  • Centralised Data Management
  • Remote Access
  • Security

What is a social network and how does it work?

A social network is a platform that connects users to build upon relationships, communication skills, and general interactions or experiences. These platforms enable the users to create profiles, and showcase information. Through features like friending, following, and messaging, users can connect and eventually create a community or a group based on their interests, hobbies, goals, and values too. An essential part of social networks are entities where it represents a person, group, or organisation. For example, a person who is using Facebook is an example of an entity, as they can interact, connect, and communicate with other users. Six degree of Separation is a vital concept related to social network, highlighting how people are connected to no more than six other users in their relationships in a social network. Digital footprints and digital citisenship are also important concepts in social networks. Digital footprint refers to an individuals left data on the internet, such as social media posts, comments, likes, dislikes, shares, and browsing history. On the other hand, digital citizenship acts upon responsible use of technology and keeping safe in the internet. It encapsulates the rights and responsibilities of users in the technological world, including privacy and security. This not only helps them to be safe but also for other users and feel as if they are getting respected. Social networks are not only vital for relationships and interactions but for also shaping a good community. Social networks whether it be face-to-face or virtually they both play a key role in our daily lives empowering connections, communication, and collaboration.
